# 3D Printing & Fabrication - Curriculum Plan

## Path Overview

**Path ID**: 3d-printing-fabrication
**Difficulty**: Intermediate
**Duration**: 8-10 weeks
**Category**: Fabrication
**Target Audience**: Ages 13+, requires CAD & 3D Design

## Description

3D Printing & Fabrication teaches the complete workflow from digital design to physical part production. Students learn about 3D printing technologies, material selection, design preparation, slicing software, and post-processing. This path enables students to take their designs and turn them into real robot components.

By completing this path, students can:
- Understand 3D printing technologies and materials
- Prepare designs for manufacturing
- Use slicing software effectively
- Optimize print settings
- Post-process and finish printed parts
- Troubleshoot printing issues

## Prerequisites

- CAD & 3D Design (ability to create 3D models)

## Level Structure

### Level 1: 3D Printing Fundamentals (Weeks 1-2)
Technologies and materials.

**Modules:**
- 3D Printing Technologies (FDM, SLA, SLS, materials)
- Materials for Robotics (filaments, properties, selection)

**Assessment**: Material and technology matching

### Level 2: Design Preparation (Weeks 3-4)
Getting designs ready for printing.

**Modules:**
- Design for 3D Printing (wall thickness, support, orientation)
- Export and Slicing (STL format, slicer software, basic settings)

**Assessment**: Design review and optimization

**Project**: Prepare design for 3D printing

### Level 3: Advanced Slicing and Settings (Weeks 5-6)
Optimizing prints for quality and speed.

**Modules:**
- Slicer Mastery (layer height, infill, speed, temperature)
- Tolerances and Precision (calibration, fit and function)

**Assessment**: Print quality analysis

**Project**: Precision part design and printing

### Level 4: Post-Processing and Quality (Weeks 7-8)
Finishing and troubleshooting.

**Modules:**
- Post-Processing Techniques (support removal, sanding, painting)
- Troubleshooting and Quality Control (failures, fixes, inspection)

**Capstone**: Complete print workflow from design to finished part

## Key Topics

### 3D Printing Technologies
- Fused Deposition Modeling (FDM/FFF)
- Stereolithography (SLA)
- Selective Laser Sintering (SLS)
- Technology comparison and selection
- Cost and time trade-offs

### Materials
- PLA and ABS filaments
- TPU and flexible materials
- High-temperature materials
- Support materials
- Biocompatible materials
- Material properties and selection

### Design Considerations
- Wall thickness requirements
- Overhangs and bridges
- Support structure design
- Part orientation
- Stress concentrations
- Assembly and fit

### Slicer Software
- Interface and workflow
- Layer height and resolution
- Infill patterns and percentage
- Support generation
- Temperature management
- Printer profiles

### Post-Processing
- Support removal methods
- Sanding and smoothing
- Painting and coating
- Vapor smoothing
- Assembly and integration
- Quality inspection
