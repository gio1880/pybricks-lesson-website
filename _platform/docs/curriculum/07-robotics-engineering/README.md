# Robotics Engineering - Curriculum Plan

## Path Overview

**Path ID**: robotics-engineering
**Difficulty**: Intermediate
**Duration**: 10-12 weeks
**Category**: Engineering
**Target Audience**: Ages 13+, requires Robotics Foundations and CAD & 3D Design

## Description

Robotics Engineering provides deep understanding of mechanical design principles applied to robots. Students learn about drivetrains, gearing systems, mechanical advantage, and the iterative engineering design process. This path bridges mechanical theory and practical robot design.

By completing this path, students can:
- Apply physics principles to robot design
- Design and analyze gear systems
- Select and design appropriate drivetrains
- Understand mechanical advantage and efficiency
- Apply the engineering design process
- Optimize robot performance

## Prerequisites

- Robotics Foundations (mechanical basics)
- CAD & 3D Design (design tools knowledge)
- High school physics recommended

## Level Structure

### Level 1: Mechanical Fundamentals (Weeks 1-2)
Physics and mechanical principles.

**Modules:**
- Forces and Motion (Newton's laws, torque, power)
- Gearing and Mechanical Advantage (gear types, ratios, efficiency)

**Assessment**: Force and torque calculations

### Level 2: Drivetrain Design (Weeks 3-4)
Designing robot movement systems.

**Modules:**
- Drivetrain Types (tank, mecanum, swerve)
- Chassis and Frame Design (structure, weight distribution)

**Assessment**: Drivetrain comparison analysis

**Project**: Design optimal drivetrain for specific task

### Level 3: Mechanisms and Attachments (Weeks 5-6)
Specialized robot subsystems.

**Modules:**
- Common Mechanisms (linkages, intake, shooter, clamping)
- Subsystem Integration (motion transmission, load distribution)

**Assessment**: Mechanism design challenge

**Project**: Design complete mechanism system

### Level 4: Engineering Process and Optimization (Weeks 7-8)
Systematic design and improvement.

**Modules:**
- Design Process (requirements, ideation, prototyping)
- Performance Analysis (measurement, testing, iteration)

**Capstone**: Complete engineering design project

## Key Topics

### Mechanical Physics
- Newton's three laws
- Torque and rotational force
- Mechanical advantage
- Friction and efficiency
- Energy and power
- Stress and strain

### Design Concepts
- Gear types and ratios
- Pulley systems
- Linkage mechanisms
- Material selection
- Tolerances and fits
- Assembly considerations

### Engineering Process
- Requirements definition
- Brainstorming and ideation
- Prototyping and testing
- Analysis and optimization
- Documentation and reporting

### Competition Applications
- Drive selection for game
- Mechanism design for objectives
- Trade-off analysis
- Reliability vs. complexity
- Team coordination

## Assessment Strategy

**Formative:**

- Prototype testing

**Summative:**
- Unit design challenges
- Engineering calculations assessments
- Design project portfolio
- Presentation and documentation
